The plan & strategy

50th Anniversary — launch campaign

Your brief calls the founding store's 50th anniversary a ready-made moment. This is that campaign, and it doubles as proof of the centralized system, because it forces one brand, one calendar, one engine across every store at once.

The moment is now: the 50th is 2026 and already being celebrated — a charity golf tournament (June 21-22, 2026) anchors it. So we amplify the 50th you're already running, not start one from scratch. (Confirm the exact founding date with Mani.)

The idea: not a sale, a thank-you. "50 years of helping you buy." The story is the reason to pay attention; the offer is the reason to act now.

Four content pillars

  • Heritage — the founder story (Sky leads the founder series).
  • Community — 50 years of charity, the golf tournament, local roots.
  • Customers — "50 years, your stories" UGC drive.
  • Product — anniversary room sets, mattresses, appliances, financing.

Three offer options (pick one or blend)

  • A — "50 Months": 50-month financing or a 50-themed credit. Leans on your strongest lever; protects margin.
  • B — "50 Ways We Give Back": a community give-back in every town. Earns goodwill a discount never will. (Recommended public face.)
  • C — "50 Years, 50 Deals": rolling anniversary bundles, co-op funded. The sales-forward lever if the date is near.

Co-op compliant. No "guaranteed approval" on financing. Charity/family figures only with sign-off.

Paid ad strategy — 13 locations

Meta-first (your order), Google later. One ad account, structured and measured per store in POS dollars via offline conversion uploads. At this budget (~$330-500/day across 13) we run CBO / Advantage+ by default — splitting that across 13 manual ABO ad sets would starve every one of them below the learning threshold. Per-store control comes from campaign-level segmentation (a campaign per store/region), not ABO ad sets. Co-op funds a real share.

CampaignOptimizationStructure
1 · Local ProspectingCBOOne campaign per store, 2-3 broad ad sets, geo-radius by store
2 · Advantage+ ShoppingAdvantage+1-2 group-wide, catalog
3 · RetargetingCBO4 warm audiences
4 · Lead Gen (financing/consult)CBOper store / pooled by region
5 · Vendor/seasonal (co-op)CBO/Adv+per event

Budget at $10k/mo: Prospecting 50% · Shopping 20% · Retargeting 15% · Lead-gen 10% · Vendor 5%. Co-op stretches it 20-40%+. Pilot 2-3 stores first at a real per-store budget (ABO reserved for clean tests or guaranteeing spend to a strategic store, and for later at scale).

Appliances handled carefully: lead with trust (warranty, delivery, reviews) and scale only after the reputation fixes. Furniture + mattress paid scale first.

Pilot store — Prince George (the flagship)

The founding store (1976), ~250k sq ft, its own warehouse, Mani's home market. The natural first pilot. Prove it here, then roll the proven version to the other 12.

The 30-day plan

  • Week 1 — Claim & clean: Google Business Profile, NAP, diagnose the findability bug, stand up the dashboard.
  • Week 2 — Repair the record: respond to the warranty/delivery reviews, turn on the post-purchase review ask, build the local page.
  • Week 3 — Turn on demand: launch local ads (prospecting + retargeting + lead-gen), publish founder + local content.
  • Week 4 — Show the numbers: first POS-dollar dashboard read, before/after on the profile and the AI answer.
